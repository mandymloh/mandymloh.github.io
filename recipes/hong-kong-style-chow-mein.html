Hong Kong Style Supreme Soy Sauce Chow Mein

August 13, 2021 by Mandy

Skip to Ingredients List
Yield: 4 servings
Prep time: 15 minutes
Cook time: 15 minutes
Total time: 30 minutes

Hong Kong Style Supreme Soy Sauce Chow Mein is a staple noodle dish that can be found at dim sum restaurants and Hong Kong Cafes. Growing up in the San Gabriel Valley, my parents took me to Hong Kong Cafes for brunch all the time. One was called OK Cafe in Monterey Park. I never knew why they were called OK Cafe because their food was LIT. The menu usually consisted of delicious comfort foods like peanut butter french toast with condensed milk, black pepper steak served on hot plates, all sorts of noodles and rice dishes, and signature coffees and milk teas. I loved ordering the soy sauce chow mein and “Lai Wan” congee with a savory fried doughnut stick. I’m salivating right now as I am writing this paragraph.

A few tips to make it Hong Kong style is to use Cantonese egg noodles The noodles are par-boiled and pan fried with oil until it has a slightly crispy golden edge. Aromatic ingredients like yellow flowering chives, sliced onions, green onions, and beansprouts are added. It is then seasoned with soy sauce and dark soy sauce mixed with a bit of water to avoid uneven coloring. I decided to add scallops and king oyster mushrooms for a hearty meal but you can customize and use your favorite protein or skip it.

Ingredients


    For the Protein

  • 4-6 scallops or your favorite protein

    • For the Veggies

  • 1/2 yellow onion (sliced)
  • 1 sweet mini red pepper (sliced thinly)
  • 2 stalks of green onions (cut into long sections)
  • 1 bunch of flowering chives
  • 1 bunch of bean sprouts
  • 4 king oyster mushrooms (sliced, optional)

    • For the Sauce

  • 3 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp dark soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp water

    • For the Noodles

  • 6 cantonese egg noodles
  • Olive oil (for frying)

Directions



Boil the noodles following package instructions and undercook it for 1 minute.
Drain the noodles and set aside.
Add enough olive oil to coat the wok or pan and let noodles cook until slightly crisp.
Set the noodles aside.



Season the scallops with 1/2 tsp of salt and coat the scallops with all purpose flour.
In a separate wok or pan, add olive oil and pan fry scallops until half cooked. Set aside.
In the same pan, add bean sprouts and chives and pan fry for a few minutes. Push aside.
Then, saute the sliced onions, green onions, and sliced king oyster mushrooms until fragrant.
Return noodles and scallops to the wok.
Add the sauce and stir fry for a couple more minutes until well combined.
Add sliced red peppers for a pop of color. Enjoy!
